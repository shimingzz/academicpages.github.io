This manuscript reviews how GNN-family models have been applied to generalized morphology-derived similarity networks (GMSNs), including MSN-GNN, MIND-GNN, gray matter similarity GNN, R2SN-GNN, radiomics similarity-network GNN, and related morphology-derived graph approaches.

The review emphasizes graph construction, validation strength, leakage control, reproducibility, interpretability, and translational risk rather than reporting accuracy alone.
